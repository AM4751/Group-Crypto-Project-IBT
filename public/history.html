What is Cryptocurrency?

Cryptocurrency is a form of digital currency that uses cryptography for secure financial transactions. It is decentralized and typically operates on a blockchain platform.

Key Events in Crypto History

  • 2008: Bitcoin whitepaper released by Satoshi Nakamoto.
  • 2009: First Bitcoin transaction and genesis block mined.
  • 2011: Rival cryptocurrencies like Litecoin emerge.
  • 2015: Ethereum launches with smart contract support.
  • 2017: Crypto market boom and ICO craze.
  • 2021: Bitcoin hits over $60,000; institutional interest grows.

Why It Matters

Cryptocurrencies represent a shift in how we store, transfer, and understand value. They enable decentralized finance (DeFi), digital ownership (NFTs), and global borderless transactions.
